Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) is a discrete-time optimization algorithm widely used in machine learning. Under the regime of a small learning rate $\eta$, its dynamics can be accurately modeled by a continuous-time stochastic differential equation (SDE), specifically the Langevin Equation. The goal of this section is to review
how SGD asymptotically converges to Langevin equation.

Consider an objective function $J(\theta)$ on $\Theta$, 
where we want to find 
the minimizer of $\theta^*$ over $\Theta$. 
In SGD setting, there are sample or training data set available:

$$J_i(\theta) = J(\theta) + \epsilon_i, \quad i = 1, 2, \ldots, n, \quad 
\forall \theta\in \Theta,$$

for a large number $n$. In the above, $\epsilon_i$ is i.i.d noise, which 
is assumed to follow a centered normal distribution with variance $\beta>0$. By Central Limit Theorem, one can write
$$ \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n J_i(\theta) \approx J(\theta) + \sqrt{\beta/n} Z, \quad Z \sim \mathcal N(0,1).$$
